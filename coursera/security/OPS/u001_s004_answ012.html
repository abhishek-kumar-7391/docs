SAQ 7

Answer

A keyspace of 56 bits provides 256≈7.2×1016 possible keys. At a rate of 1012 keys per second it would take approximately 7.2×104 seconds or about 20 hours to try every key. A keyspace of 128 bits provides 2128≈3.4×1038 possible keys. This would take approximately 3.4×1026 seconds or about 1019 years. (Note: the lifetime to date of the universe is thought to be of the order of 1010 years.)
